## **BUSINESS CONCEPT UNDER ANALYSIS**

**SalesSherpa:** AI sales assistant that provides territory analysis, prospect research, pipeline management, and automated follow-up specifically for B2B sales reps. Pricing: $99-299/month individual subscriptions, with enterprise team plans available.

---

## **STEP 1 ‚Äî VIABILITY CHECK**

### **Does this solve a real problem? Rating: 7/10**
**REASONING:**
- **Real pain exists:** B2B sales reps spend 65% of time on research, admin work vs actual selling
- **Clear inefficiency:** Manual prospect research, territory analysis, follow-up tracking
- **Emotional frustration:** Reps losing deals due to poor preparation, missed follow-ups
- **BUT market saturated:** Hundreds of existing sales tools, CRM platforms, automation solutions

### **Will people pay for this? Rating: 5/10**
**REASONING:**
- **Individual reps hesitant:** Most expect companies to pay for sales tools, not personal expense
- **Budget competition:** $99-299/month competes with rent, car payments, personal expenses
- **ROI unclear:** Hard to prove direct revenue impact from sales intelligence vs actual selling
- **Enterprise potential:** Companies might pay for team licenses, but crowded competitive landscape

### **Can this scale? Rating: 4/10**  
**REASONING:**
- **High customer acquisition costs:** B2B sales reps are expensive to reach and convert
- **Churn risk high:** Easy to cancel if not seeing immediate results
- **Feature commoditization:** Core features (CRM, research, automation) available in existing platforms
- **Competitive pressure:** Salesforce, HubSpot, Outreach adding AI features continuously

### **Overall Viability Score: 5.3/10**
**ASSESSMENT:** Solves real problem but in an oversaturated market with unclear differentiation and challenging unit economics.

---

## **STEP 2 ‚Äî THE 5 WAYS SALESSHERPA DIES**

### **Death Scenario 1: Market Risk - "Reps won't pay personally"**
**What happens:** Individual sales reps refuse to pay $99-299/month from personal funds, expect company to provide tools  
**Likelihood:** HIGH  
**Why:** Sales tools traditionally purchased by companies, not individual expense  
**Prevention:** Pivot to enterprise B2B sales, demonstrate ROI to sales managers

### **Death Scenario 2: Execution Risk - "Data quality not good enough for sales decisions"**
**What happens:** AI provides inaccurate prospect information, reps make bad calls, lose deals, blame SalesSherpa  
**Likelihood:** MEDIUM  
**Why:** Sales data is complex, constantly changing, high stakes for accuracy  
**Prevention:** Multiple data sources, confidence scoring, human verification layer

### **Death Scenario 3: Competition Risk - "Salesforce crushes us with Einstein AI"**
**What happens:** Major CRM platforms add similar AI features, customers prefer integrated solutions  
**Likelihood:** HIGH  
**Why:** Salesforce, HubSpot have resources, existing customer base, integration advantages  
**Prevention:** Focus on features big platforms ignore, superior user experience, specialization

### **Death Scenario 4: Financial Risk - "Customer acquisition cost exceeds lifetime value"**
**What happens:** Cost to acquire sales reps exceeds revenue from subscriptions, unsustainable unit economics  
**Likelihood:** HIGH  
**Why:** Sales reps expensive to reach, high churn in sales tools market  
**Prevention:** Referral programs, content marketing, freemium model, enterprise focus

### **Death Scenario 5: Timing Risk - "Market already saturated with sales tools"**  
**What happens:** Sales reps suffer from tool fatigue, resist adopting another platform  
**Likelihood:** MEDIUM  
**Why:** Average sales rep uses 6+ tools already, integration headaches common  
**Prevention:** Replace existing tools instead of adding to stack, superior consolidation

## **STEP 4 ‚Äî COMPETITIVE MOAT ASSESSMENT**

### **What stops someone from copying this in 6 months?**
- **Very little:** AI sales tools are commodity market
- **No proprietary data:** Sales intelligence available from multiple sources  
- **Low switching costs:** Easy for reps to try competitors
- **Integration challenges:** Hard to build deep CRM integrations

### **Defensibility Rating: WEAK**
**Why it's weak:**
- **Technology not proprietary:** AI sales features becoming standard
- **Network effects minimal:** Each rep's usage independent
- **Customer switching costs low:** Sales reps change tools frequently
- **Major platforms adding features:** Salesforce Einstein, HubSpot AI

### **What would make the moat stronger?**
- **Exclusive data partnerships:** Unique information sources competitors can't access
- **Deep CRM integrations:** Become embedded in sales workflows
- **Sales team network effects:** Team collaboration features, shared insights
- **Industry specialization:** Focus on specific verticals with unique needs
